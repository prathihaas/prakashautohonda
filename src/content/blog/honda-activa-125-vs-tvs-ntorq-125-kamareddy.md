## Engine & Performance

### Honda Activa 125

Honda's PGM-FI (Programmed Fuel Injection) engine is tuned for efficiency and refinement — not outright speed. The 8.18 bhp output might seem modest on paper, but in real-world Kamareddy riding it's more than enough.

**What you feel**: Buttery smooth throttle response, zero jerks at slow speeds (important in Kamareddy town traffic), and consistent performance in Telangana's summer heat.

**Highway performance**: On the Kamareddy-Nizamabad highway (65km stretch), the Activa 125 cruises comfortably at 70–75 kmph without feeling strained.

### TVS NTorq 125

The NTorq's Race XConnect engine makes 9.4 bhp — noticeably more than Activa 125. It's peppier off the line, with a sportier character throughout the rev range.

**What you feel**: More acceleration when you twist the throttle, a slightly aggressive engine note. Young riders love this energy.

**The trade-off**: That extra performance costs fuel. NTorq returns 42–45 kmpl vs Activa's 50–52 kmpl — a significant difference over the life of the scooter.

## Comfort & Ride Quality

**Honda Activa 125**: Designed for all-day comfort. The wide, cushioned seat, upright riding posture, and compliant suspension make it the better choice for long rides like Kamareddy to Nizamabad (65km). Handles speed bumps and potholed village roads near Pitlam and Bibipet without drama.

**TVS NTorq 125**: Stiffer suspension tuned for sporty riding. Excellent at higher speeds but can feel firm over rough village roads. Heavier at 118kg vs Activa's 110kg — noticeable when parking or maneuvering.

For Kamareddy's mixed urban-rural road conditions, Activa 125's softer, more forgiving setup is better suited.
