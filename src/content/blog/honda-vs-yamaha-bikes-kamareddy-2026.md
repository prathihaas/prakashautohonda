## Head-to-Head Comparisons

### 1. Commuter Segment: Honda SP 160 vs Yamaha FZ-S V3

| | Honda SP 160 | Yamaha FZ-S V3 |
|--|-------------|----------------|
| Engine | 160.6cc FI | 149cc FI |
| Power | 14.73 bhp | 12.4 bhp |
| Mileage | ~55 kmpl | ~45 kmpl |
| Weight | 156 kg | 139 kg |
| Ex-showroom | ~₹1,23,000 | ~₹1,20,000 |
| Character | Commuter-sports | Sporty-aggressive |
| Seat height | 790 mm | 795 mm |

**Analysis for Kamareddy buyers**:

The FZ-S is lighter and has a more aggressive, sporty character. Young riders in Kamareddy love the FZ-S's muscular stance and involving ride feel.

The SP 160 has more power (14.73 vs 12.4 bhp) and significantly better mileage (55 vs 45 kmpl). On the Kamareddy-Nizamabad highway (65km), SP 160 saves:
- Monthly fuel (1,500 km): SP 160 ₹3,054 vs FZ-S ₹3,733
- Annual saving: ~₹8,148

Over 3 years: SP 160 saves ₹24,444 in fuel vs FZ-S — more than recovering the ₹3,000 price premium.

**Verdict**: For mileage and long-distance commuting, **SP 160 wins**. For pure sporty fun and urban riding, **FZ-S V3** has more character.

### 2. Performance Segment: Honda Hornet 2.0 vs Yamaha FZ25

| | Honda Hornet 2.0 | Yamaha FZ25 |
|--|----------------|-------------|
| Engine | 184.4cc FI | 249cc FI |
| Power | 17.03 bhp | 20.8 bhp |
| Mileage | ~45 kmpl | ~40 kmpl |
| Ex-showroom | ~₹1,36,000 | ~₹1,53,000 |
| Character | Sports-commuter | Mid-size sportster |

**Analysis**:

The FZ25 is a bigger bike with more displacement (249cc) and power (20.8 bhp) but costs ₹17,000 more. For riders wanting pure performance, the FZ25 is genuinely more exciting.

However, the Hornet 2.0's 184.4cc is more than adequate for Kamareddy's roads. Better mileage (45 vs 40 kmpl), lower price, and Honda's reliability make it a stronger practical choice.

**Verdict**: FZ25 for performance enthusiasts. **Hornet 2.0** for performance with practicality.

**Analysis for Kamareddy roads**:

The R15 V4 is a proper supersport-inspired motorcycle — the closest you can get to a track bike at this price. But on Kamareddy's roads, the R15's racing crouch is fatiguing for daily commuting, and its 170mm ground clearance scrapes more often than CB200X's 200mm.

The CB200X is designed for Kamareddy's road reality — mixed highways and rough village roads. Better ground clearance, upright comfort, and significantly lower price. Plus, CB200X is more versatile for both commuting and weekend adventures.

**Verdict for Kamareddy**: **Honda CB200X** is better suited to Telangana's mixed roads. R15 is for pure sport enthusiasts who rarely venture beyond smooth highways.

Fascino is narrower and stylish — popular among women riders for its slim, stylish profile and lighter weight.

**Verdict**: Fascino 125 wins on mileage, weight, and price. **Activa 125** wins on resale value, brand trust, and warranty coverage. Most Kamareddy buyers still choose Activa for long-term ownership confidence.
