## Mileage & Performance — The Fuel Injection Difference

This comparison comes down to one key technical difference: **Honda SP 125 and Hero Glamour 125 use fuel injection; Bajaj Pulsar 125 uses a carburetor.**

Why does this matter for Kamareddy buyers?

**Fuel injection advantages in Telangana conditions:**
- Consistent fuel delivery regardless of temperature (cold mornings in December, hot summer afternoons)
- Better cold-start performance — no need to choke
- More consistent mileage across different altitudes and conditions
- Lower long-term maintenance as injectors require less service than carburetors

**Honda SP 125 real-world mileage in Telangana:** Riders from Kamareddy, Nizamabad, and Banswada, Nizamabad, Yellareddy, Bheemgal, Balkonda, and Armoor consistently report **55–62 kmpl** in mixed use. The PGM-FI system (Honda's proprietary fuel injection) is the most refined in this class.

**Hero Glamour 125 real-world mileage:** Reports from Telangana show **50–54 kmpl** — decent but notably lower than Honda's claim and Honda's real-world performance. The Glamour uses a different tuning of fuel injection that prioritizes performance slightly over efficiency.

**Bajaj Pulsar 125 real-world mileage:** Despite the sporty looks, real-world figures are **45–50 kmpl** in Kamareddy conditions. The carburetor setup requires occasional tuning, and performance degrades between service intervals. At ₹105/litre petrol, this means ~₹8,000–₹12,000 more fuel cost over 3 years vs Honda SP 125.

**Performance on Kamareddy roads:**
The Bajaj Pulsar 125's 11.8 bhp engine revs higher and delivers a sportier character — riders who enjoy spirited riding may prefer it. But for daily commuting in Kamareddy and weekend family rides, the Honda SP 125's torquey, smooth delivery is more practical.
