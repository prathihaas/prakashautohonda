## Understanding Two-Wheeler Loan Structure

A two-wheeler loan is a simple installment loan. You borrow a fixed amount from a lender (bank or NBFC), agree to repay it over a set period (tenure), and pay interest on the borrowed amount.

**Key terms to understand:**

- **Principal**: The loan amount (ex-showroom price minus down payment)
- **Interest rate**: Annual rate charged by the lender (typically 11–16% for two-wheelers in 2026)
- **Tenure**: Loan duration (12 to 48 months typically)
- **EMI**: Equated Monthly Installment — your fixed monthly payment
- **Processing fee**: One-time fee charged by lender (₹500–₹2,000 typically)
- **Foreclosure charges**: Fee for closing loan early (1–5% of outstanding amount)

## EMI Calculation Formula

EMI = [P × R × (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N - 1]

Where:
- P = Principal amount
- R = Monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100)
- N = Tenure in months

**Practical Example:**

Honda SP 125 (₹95,000 ex-showroom):
- Down payment: ₹20,000
- Loan amount (P): ₹75,000
- Interest rate: 12% p.a. (R = 1% per month)
- Tenure (N): 36 months

EMI = [75,000 × 0.01 × (1.01)^36] / [(1.01)^36 - 1]
EMI = [75,000 × 0.01 × 1.4308] / [0.4308]
EMI ≈ ₹2,490/month

For Honda Activa 125 loan of ₹73,600 at 12% interest:

| Tenure | EMI | Total Paid | Interest Paid |

| 24 months | ₹3,470 | ₹83,280 | ₹9,680 |

| 48 months | ₹1,937 | ₹92,976 | ₹19,376 |

**Key insight**: Longer tenure = lower EMI but significantly more interest paid. If you can manage the higher EMI, shorter tenure saves money.

## Eligibility Criteria for Kamareddy Buyers

### Salaried Individuals
- Minimum age: 21 years

- Employment stability: 6+ months at current employer

## After Loan Sanction — Important Notes

- **Insurance is mandatory**: Comprehensive insurance must be taken (can be included in loan or paid separately)
- **Registration cost**: Typically ₹3,000–₹5,000 depending on engine size
- **Hypothecation**: The bike's RC book will show the lender's name until loan is fully repaid
- **NOC after full payment**: Get the No Objection Certificate from lender and update RC book — keep this document safely
